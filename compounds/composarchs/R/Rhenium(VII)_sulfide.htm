Rhenium(VII) sulfide

Rhenium(VII) sulfide is a chemical compound with the formula Re2S7. It can be produced through the reaction of ReO4− and H2S in 4N HCl.[1]

Synthesis
  • Direct combination of rhenium and sulfur:
2 R e + 7 S   → Δ   R e 2 S 7 {\displaystyle \mathrm {2Re+7S\ {\xrightarrow {\Delta }}\ Re_{2}S_{7}} }
  • Treating rhenium(VII) oxide with hydrogen sulfide:
R e 2 O 7 + 7 H 2 S   → Δ   R e 2 S 7 + 7 H 2 O {\displaystyle \mathrm {Re_{2}O_{7}+7H_{2}S\ {\xrightarrow {\Delta }}\ Re_{2}S_{7}+7H_{2}O} }

Reactions

Rhenium(VII) sulfide decomposes when heated in vacuum:

R e 2 S 7   → 600 o C   2 R e S 2 + 3 S {\displaystyle \mathrm {Re_{2}S_{7}\ {\xrightarrow {600^{o}C}}\ 2ReS_{2}+3S} }

It is converted to oxide when heated in air:

2 R e 2 S 7 + 21 O 2   → Δ   2 R e 2 O 7 + 14 S O 2 {\displaystyle \mathrm {2Re_{2}S_{7}+21O_{2}\ {\xrightarrow {\Delta }}\ 2Re_{2}O_{7}+14SO_{2}} }
