Iron(II) acetate

Iron(II) acetate is a coordination complex with formula Fe(C2H3O2)2. It is a white solid, although impure samples can be slightly colored. A light green tetrahydrate is also known, which is highly soluble in water.

Preparation and structure

Iron powder reacts with hot acetic acid to give the product:[1]

Fe + 2 CH3CO2H → Fe(CH3CO2)2 + H2

It can also be made from the insoluble, olive green, Iron(II) carbonate.

It adopts a polymeric structure with octahedral Fe(II) centers bridged by acetate ligands. It is not an ionic compound.[1]

The hydrate can be made by the reaction of ferrous oxide or ferrous hydroxide with acetic acid.[5]

Reaction of scrap iron with acetic acid affords a brown mixture of various iron(II) and iron(III) acetates that are used in dyeing.[6]

Uses

Ferrous acetate is used as a mordant by the dye industry. Ebonizing wood is one such process.[7]
