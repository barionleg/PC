Benzyl cyanide

Benzyl cyanide (abbreviated BnCN) is an organic compound with the chemical formula C6H5CH2CN. This colorless oily aromatic liquid is an important precursor to numerous compounds in organic chemistry.[2]

Preparation and reactions

Benzyl cyanide can be produced via Kolbe nitrile synthesis between benzyl chloride and sodium cyanide[3] and by oxidative decarboxylation of phenylalanine.[4]

Benzyl cyanide undergoes many reactions characteristic of nitriles. Hydrolyzed gives phenylacetic acid[5] or used in the Pinner reaction to yield phenylacetic acid esters.[6] Hydrogenation gives Î²-phenethylamine.[7]

The compound contains an "active methylene unit". Bromination occurs gives PhCHBrCN.[8] A variety of base-induced reactions results in the formation of new carbon-carbon bonds.[9][10][11]

Uses

Benzyl cyanide is used as a solvent[12] and as a starting material in the synthesis of fungicides,[13] fragrances (phenethyl alcohol), antibiotics,[2] and other pharmaceuticals. The partial hydrolysis of BnCN results in 2-phenylacetamide,[14] a known anticonvulsant.

Pharmaceuticals

Benzyl cyanide is a useful precursor to numerous pharmaceuticals. Examples include:

  • Anorectics (e.g. sibutramine)[15]
  • Analgesics (e.g. ethoheptazine, ketobemidone, pethidine, and phenoperidine)[15]
  • Antiarrhythmics (e.g. disopyramide)[15]
  • Antidepressants (e.g. venlafaxine)[15]
  • Antihistamines (e.g. levocabastine and chlorphenamine)[15][16]
  • Antimalarial medications (e.g. pyrimethamine)[15]
  • Antitussives (e.g. isoaminile, oxeladin, butethamate, pentapiperide, and pentoxyverine)[17]
  • Diuretics (e.g. triamterene)[18]
  • Hypnotics (e.g. alonimid and phenobarbital)[15][19]
  • Spasmolytics (e.g. pentapiperide and drofenine)[15][20]
  • Stimulants (e.g. methylphenidate)[15]
  • Azatadine

Regulation

Because benzyl cyanide is a useful precursor to numerous drugs with recreational use potential, many countries strictly regulate the compound.

United States

Benzyl cyanide is regulated in the United States as a DEA List I chemical.

Safety

Benzyl cyanide, like related benzyl derivatives, is an irritant to the skin and eyes.[2]
