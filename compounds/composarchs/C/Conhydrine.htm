Conhydrine

Conhydrine is a poisonous alkaloid found in poison hemlock (Conium maculatum) in small quantities.

Isolation and properties

This oxygenated alkaloid was isolated by Wertheim from C. maculatum.[1] It crystallises in colourless leaflets, has a coniine-like odour, can be sublimed, and is strongly basic. It crystallises readily from ether. The salts are crystalline; the aurichloride small rhombs or prisms, mp. 133 Â°C; the benzoyl derivative mp. 132 Â°C.
